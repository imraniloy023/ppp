Research & Projects

Disquietous: Weird analogies/Queer technologies

Concept development, research, and curatorial direction

A yearlong curatorial intensive with a lecture series, discussions, exhibition, and publication at the intersection of digital lives and gender/sexual/bodily expressions with support from the Numun Fund.

Cinema Palestine Bangladesh

Concept development and curatorial direction, programming and organizing

A film series with cinema screenings, director talk, discussion, and teach-in.

Nothing And Too Much All at the Same Time

Compilation of a dossier, workshop and exhibition participation

A multi-format intervention organized by the Artist Project Group around a thematic question – What Can Artists Do Now? Beginning with a workshop and discussion on the role of “consultants” and “consulting services” and their relation to artistic practice and capitalism, the intervention culminated in an exhibition.

Mass + Produce

Concept development, production and publication, exhibition participation

A booklet which looks at the ecological, community, political aspects of food production, agriculture, distribution networks, and consumption, in other words, food as praxis, food as political. Produced as part of Goethe-Institut’s Futures Beyond the Self call with an exhibition to conclude the project.

Longing and Belonging

Concept development, research, and programming

An oral history and “histories from the ground” project assembling narratives, conversations, interviews from families and their descendants of Indian émigrés in Dhaka, Bangladesh affected by the 1947 partition of the Indian subcontinent. Supported by the Goethe-Institut’s Inherited Memories program.

Fresh Cuts

Contribution of text and images

A volume on the “unspoken energies of desire” titled Happy Hypocrite – Silver Bandage (Issue 11) by commissioning artist and editor Erica Scourti published by Book Works.

i/C/U: Connection & Control

Workshop and exhibition participation, contribution to booklet

Organized and spearheaded by home school pdx and curator Hiba Ali, a reading group, workshop, and exhibition on surveillance technologies and ways to subvert them.

psychic disturbances

Artist residency, research, and exhibition

A mixed-media installation titled “psychic disturbances: airport series” exhibited as part of the residency at TIFA Working Studios. The exhibit was produced from research, primary interviews, and archival exploration of land rights and social movement against the proposed Pune international airport in the city’s outskirts that could displace seven villages in Purandar.
